ThinkMate

An AI-Powered Socratic Tutoring Platform for Developing Critical Thinking Skills Across Two Pilot Disciplines

UAEU CETL Innovation Project Engineering + Humanities May – December 2026
Phase: Pre-Pilot Development

Why ThinkMate?

Bridging the gap between content knowledge and applied reasoning

The Challenge

Critical thinking is a formal learning outcome in most degree programmes, yet performance remains uneven. Students complete coursework successfully while struggling to justify claims, evaluate alternatives, and identify hidden assumptions. In classes of 40–80 students, sustained individualised Socratic questioning is not feasible through instructor time alone.

Our Approach

ThinkMate is a theory-constrained, reasoning-first AI tutoring platform. It separates pedagogical control from language generation: a rule-based move selector chooses the next Socratic move, and only then does the LLM generate the question. A safeguard layer audits every turn for answer leakage. The result is structured dialogue that pushes students toward deeper reasoning without giving them the answer.

How It Works

A four-stage adaptive dialogue cycle

1

Student Modelling

Assesses the learner's current cognitive state and Bloom's taxonomy level from their response

2

Strategy Selection

Rule-based move selector picks from six Socratic moves targeting detected Paul-Elder weaknesses

3

Dialogue Generation

LLM generates the contextual Socratic question within constrained prompt templates

4

Safeguard Audit

Response is checked for answer leakage and factual accuracy before delivery to the student

Grounded in Theory

Three linked frameworks underpin every tutoring interaction

📊

Bloom's Revised Taxonomy

Cognitive-level progression from Remember through Create. The system targets the boundary between understanding and analysing, where CT development is most critical.

🧠

Paul-Elder CT Model

Six intellectual standards — clarity, accuracy, depth, breadth, logic, fairness — operationalised as tutoring-move tags for every dialogue turn.

💬

ICAP Framework

Engagement classification (Passive, Active, Constructive, Interactive) logged per turn to explain mechanisms behind positive or null learning outcomes.
